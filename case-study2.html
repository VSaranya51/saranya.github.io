Case Study 2

Tee Time Booking & Partner Operations Platform

Focus: Lifecycle ownership, payments, external stakeholders, operational scale

Product Type: Internal + Partner-facing booking & operations platform
Users: Sales, Marketing, Operations, Finance, External Partners (Hotels & Golf Courses)

Context

As booking volumes grew across golf breaks, society golf, tours, holidays, and events, the business required a unified platform to manage:

  • Booking confirmation
  • Customer payments
  • Supplier settlements
  • Fulfilment communication
  • Revenue reporting
  • External partner visibility

This evolved from an internal booking tool into a multi-sided operational platform.

End-to-End Lifecycle Framing

The product was designed around a complete booking lifecycle:

  • Booking Created
  • Deposit Collected
  • Partner Visibility
  • Supplier Settlement
  • Confirmation & Vouchers
  • KPI & Revenue Reporting

My responsibility was ensuring this lifecycle was:

  • Reliable
  • Transparent
  • Financially accurate
  • Scalable

Internal Booking & Sales Management

Sales and marketing teams could:

  • Create bookings across all product categories
  • Manage group details, dates, and itinerary components
  • Track booking status
  • Monitor payment progress

The system centralised operational workflows that were previously fragmented across emails and spreadsheets.

Payments & Financial Flows

A critical layer of this system was money movement.

The platform supported:

  • Customer deposit collection via payment API
  • Payment status tracking
  • Supplier and business partner settlements
  • Reduced manual reconciliation

Key Considerations

  • Payment failures
  • Partial payments
  • Refunds & cancellations
  • Financial traceability

This required collaboration with finance to ensure settlement logic aligned with contracts and reporting needs.

External Partner Portal (Hotel & Golf Course Managers)

As bookings scaled, external partners required visibility into:

  • Confirmed bookings
  • Dates, group sizes, and product types
  • Cancellations or amendments
  • Payment status
  • Historical booking volume

Problem

Previously:

  • Information was shared via email
  • Confirmation via Email or Phone
  • Manual reconciliation was required
  • Budgeting and contract forecasting lacked transparency

Solution

We introduced a secure Partner Portal allowing authorised hotel and golf course managers to:

  • View bookings generated by partner
  • Track updates and cancellations
  • Monitor payment and settlement status
  • Access historical booking data

Business Value

For partners:

  • Improved marketing budgeting and capacity planning
  • Better contract alignment
  • Reduced communication friction

For internal teams:

  • Fewer operational queries
  • Improved partner relationships
  • Stronger commercial trust

This transformed the product into a B2B collaboration platform, not just an internal system.

Fulfilment & Communication Automation

The platform automated:

  • Booking confirmation emails
  • Itineraries
  • Voucher generation

This reduced manual follow-up and ensured consistency in customer communications.

Performance & Revenue Dashboards

The system provided visibility into:

Marketing KPIs

  • Lead sources
  • Campaign performance

Sales KPIs

  • Bookings per product category
  • Conversion rates

Revenue KPIs

  • Deposits collected
  • Outstanding balances
  • Total revenue

Leadership gained real-time visibility into performance, enabling data-driven decisions.

Risks & Trade-offs

Operational Risks

  • Payment inconsistencies
  • Supplier settlement errors
  • Data inaccuracies
  • Cancellation policy misalignment

Trade-offs Made

  • Prioritised deposit collection before full automation
  • Focused on operational stability before customer self-service
  • Introduced partner visibility in phases to manage data sensitivity

Delivery Approach

  • Agile / Scrum methodology
  • Risk-heavy flows (payments) prioritised first
  • Cross-functional workshops with finance, sales, marketing and operations
  • Iterative release of partner portal features

Outcomes & Impact

  • Centralised booking and payment management
  • Reduced manual operational effort
  • Improved financial transparency
  • Strengthened external partner trust
  • Enabled scalable operational growth

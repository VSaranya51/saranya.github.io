Case Study 1

AI-Assisted Sales Recommendation

Sample PRD (anonymised) for an internal sales quotation platform that automates itinerary pricing and supports data-driven package recommendations.

Back to Portfolio →

Document type: Sample PRD (anonymised)

Product: Internal Sales Quotation Platform

Audience: Engineering, Sales, Stakeholders

1. Overview

Sales teams use the platform to assemble tailored golf travel packages by combining travel dates, golf course and tee-time availability, accommodation, and transportation for both domestic and international travellers, including golfers and non-golfers. The product supports multiple offerings such as inbound and outbound golf tours, golf breaks, and holiday packages.

An AI-assisted recommendation capability suggests the most suitable package combinations based on customer requirements including group size, budget, destination, dates, and preferences. These recommendations support sales decision-making by enabling quick validation and comparison of options, improving consistency and significantly reducing quote turnaround and conversion time.

2. Problem Statement

Pricing and availability for golf courses, accommodation, and transportation are spread across disconnected sources such as PDFs, spreadsheets, websites, and emails. Sales teams must manually reconcile this information to create quotes and itineraries, making the process slow and error-prone.

Frequent changes in customer requirements or availability lead to repeated quote revisions, further increasing turnaround time. The absence of a unified system for pricing, availability, and recommendations reduces sales efficiency and delays conversion.

3. Goal

Enable sales teams to generate automated total pricing for itineraries and receive data-driven recommendations, reducing quotation time and accelerating conversion.

4. Success Metrics

  • Reduction in average time to generate a quote
  • Adoption rate of AI recommendations by sales users
  • Reduction in number of quote revisions

5. Users

  • Primary: Sales Executives
  • Secondary: Account Managers

6. In Scope

  • Capture structured customer requirements
  • Generate automated itinerary pricing and ranked recommendations based on availability, pricing, and suitability patterns
  • Display recommendations with clear explanation indicators to support sales decision-making

7. Out of Scope

  • Fully automated decision-making
  • Customer-facing recommendation flows
  • Payment or booking confirmation

8. Assumptions & Risks

Assumptions

  • Required pricing data is available and accessible from source systems
  • Tee times, accommodation, and transportation availability can be reliably retrieved or validated

Risks

  • Over-reliance on recommendations without sufficient contextual review
  • Data quality issues, including incorrect or outdated rates
  • Availability mismatches across golf courses, accommodation, and transportation providers

9. Acceptance Criteria (High Level)

  • Recommendations align with provided customer inputs and defined business rules
  • Pricing is accurately calculated based on available and validated rate data
  • System responses meet agreed performance thresholds to support efficient quote generation

User Stories

User Story 1 — Capture Customer Requirements

As a Sales Executive
I want to capture structured customer details, travel dates, and itinerary preferences
So that automated total pricing for the itinerary can be generated

Acceptance Criteria

  • Given I enter customer details including group size, travel dates, selected golf courses, and accommodation preferences
  • When I submit the form
  • Then the system validates and securely stores the inputs for quotation generation

User Story 2 — Select Product Type (Inbound / Outbound)

As a Sales Executive
I want to select whether the booking is an inbound or outbound package
So that the system displays the appropriate form fields and requirements for the selected product type

Acceptance Criteria

  • Given I am creating a new quotation
  • When I select inbound or outbound as the product type
  • Then the system displays the relevant fields required for that package
  • And only applicable inputs can be entered for the selected product type

User Story 3 — Review Itinerary & Pricing Before Export

As a Sales Executive
I want to review the generated itinerary and total pricing
So that I can validate details and make adjustments before exporting the quote

Acceptance Criteria

  • Given an itinerary and pricing have been generated
  • When I open the review view
  • Then I can see a consolidated summary of itinerary components and total pricing
  • And I can make edits before finalising the quotation

User Story 4 — Explore Options via AI Chat Recommendation

As a Sales Executive
I want to explore itinerary and pricing options using an AI-assisted chat interface
So that I can quickly evaluate alternatives and respond to customer queries

Acceptance Criteria

  • Given customer requirements have been captured
  • When I interact with the AI chat to explore options (e.g. alternative courses, dates, or budgets)
  • Then the system suggests relevant itinerary options based on availability, pricing, and suitability
  • And recommendations are contextual and aligned with existing customer inputs
  • And I can apply suggested options to the quotation for further review

User Story 5 — Export Quotation as PDF or Excel

As a Sales Executive
I want to export the finalised itinerary and pricing as a PDF or Excel file
So that I can share the quotation with customers and internal stakeholders

Acceptance Criteria

  • Given the itinerary and pricing have been reviewed and approved
  • When I choose to export the quotation
  • Then the system generates a downloadable PDF or Excel file
  • And the exported document includes itinerary details, pricing breakdown, and totals
  • And the format is consistent and suitable for customer communication
